# Trademarks and Branding

This document explains the boundary between the **source license** and
**trademark/branding rights**. They are separate. Having access to the source
does not grant any right to the project's name, identity, or branding.

> Not legal advice. This describes intent. Formal trademark protection and
> enforcement should be handled with a qualified professional.

## Protected marks and identity

The following are **not licensed** for commercial use, and are not licensed for
any use that implies endorsement or official status, without prior written
permission from Patternity:

- the name **"Patternity"**;
- the name **"Food Assistant"** as used by this project, and related product
  names;
- Patternity and Food Assistant **logos, wordmarks, and visual identity**;
- overall project **branding, look, and naming**.

## What the source license does and does not grant

- The [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE) grants **copyright and patent** permissions for the
  software, for non-commercial purposes only.
- The [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE) does **not** grant any **trademark** rights. Code
  availability does not grant name or branding rights.

## Permitted references

You may use the names **descriptively and truthfully** — for example, to state
that your fork is "based on Patternity's Food Assistant research prototype" —
provided you:

- do **not** imply endorsement, sponsorship, or official status;
- do **not** use the names or logos as the name/branding of your own product or
  service;
- do **not** use them in a way likely to cause confusion about origin.

## Forks and derivatives

If you create a non-commercial fork (as permitted by [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE)),
please **rename** it and use your own branding. Do not present a fork as the
official Patternity project or the official Food Assistant product.
